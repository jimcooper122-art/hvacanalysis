Gas systems are permanently capped

With no energy points, gas buildings max out at 50 MLI points → 40-year amortization. ASHP standard reaches 85 pts → 45yr. ASHP L3 reaches 100 pts → 50yr. The resulting cash flow gap between gas and ASHP L3 ranges from $12,388/yr (furnaces) to $16,108/yr (boiler w/ landlord gas) — $124K–$161K over 10 years — on the same property, same rents, driven by MLI amortization and operating cost differences.

Tenant utility cost matters for rent sustainability

Gas furnaces cost tenants ~$7,600/yr in utilities vs. ~$3,200/yr in the ASHP L3 building. At Toronto rental market rents, higher utility costs make rent increases harder to sustain. The ASHP L3 envelope's $4,400/yr tenant utility saving provides a genuine competitive advantage in tenant retention and rent growth. Note: boiler landlord pays ~$3,920/yr space heat gas separately, making its true total cost higher than the furnace system despite lower tenant utility charges.

Toronto Hydro Multi-Meter Issue — Do Not Budget Yet

Toronto Hydro typically does not allow a single solar system to feed multiple individual meters. A sixplex with 6 separate meters requires a commercial interconnection structure. Polaron Solar is investigating. Do not include solar in construction budget until resolved. If solved via commercial program, economics are modest: ~11yr payback on $33K gross solar cost at current utility rates. Commercial rebates (potentially 30–40% of gross cost) not yet quantified by Polaron.
